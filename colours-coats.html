Breed guide

Tonkinese colours and coat types

Tonkinese colouring is often described through a combination of colour, pattern, and coat appearance. Kittens can change in shade as they mature.

Coat patterns

PatternAppearance
Natural minkA balanced contrast between body and points, traditionally associated with aqua eyes.
SolidMore even body colour with stronger contrast at the points; eye colour is commonly green.
PointedClear contrast between the lighter body and darker points; eye colour is commonly blue.

Common colour families

Colour familyGeneral description
NaturalWarm brown-toned points with a cream-to-fawn body.
BlueCool blue-grey points with a pale, blue-toned body.
ChocolateMilk-chocolate-toned points with an ivory body.
LilacSoft pink-grey points with a pale magnolia body.
Red, cream and tortieWarmer red or cream variations; tortie combines red/cream with another base colour.

Colour terminology and recognition can vary by registry. Ask about the registered colour and the kitten’s parentage for a precise description.

Coat care

The Tonkinese coat is typically short, close-lying, and low-maintenance. Gentle weekly grooming helps remove loose hair and is a useful chance to check skin, ears, and nails.
